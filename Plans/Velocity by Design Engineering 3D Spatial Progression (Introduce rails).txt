Tying a passive gain to movement effectively turns traversal into your core active gameplay. This successfully treats active play as a rewarding accelerant to their baseline progression rather than a strict requirement
. However, because you want the player to move extremely fast without the game becoming unplayable, you must carefully manage how that speed scales.
Here is how to balance and structure this high-speed, movement-based system:
1. Shift from Manual Steering to a "Logistics Puzzle" A common pitfall in 3D incrementals is that navigating a map manually can quickly become a frustrating chore
. If a player is moving at breakneck speeds, forcing them to manually steer through a fixed-camera view will make the game uncontrollable. Instead, treat the permanent infrastructure like a logistics and flow puzzle
.
Allow the placed infrastructure (such as Speed Tracks, rails) to guide or automate the player's high-speed movement. The challenge then shifts away from twitch reflexes and becomes about designing the most efficient, continuous spatial loop to maximize their passive movement gains
.

Floating joystick: what should the tap mean?

Each option starts movement from the place your thumb touches, but they assign meaning to that first tap differently.

ACT

A. Tap sets joystick center

Your first touch only creates the joystick. Drag direction controls movement. The ACT button still handles interact/gather.

tap target
ACT

B. Tap implies intent

Short taps near objects interact with them; holding and dragging turns that same touch into the floating joystick.

left or right thumb

C. Any-side thumb mode

First touch on either lower side creates movement. A tap-without-drag can act/interact, so one-hand play works from either side.

My recommendation

Use B as the default. It makes the tap location meaningful without turning the game into tap-to-move: quick tap means interact when there is something usable nearby, drag means move. Keep the explicit ACT button for precision and as a fallback.
